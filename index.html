Tim Stevens

Full Stack Web Developer

My Elevator Pitch...

My professional experience includes fisheries biology, operations management, information systems, software release management, testing, support, deployment of SaaS platforms, and software product management. In each role I gained experience, perspective, and skills that equipped me for the next. After fifteen years of wearing various information technology hats I realized full-stack web development was the next right step. Two months into my training as a full-stack web developer, it became obvious this was the right move. Although presently junior in my coding skills, I bring a boatload of valuable experience and perspective to the local dev environment.

Imagine a software engineer who has been virtually every type of software stakeholder - business analyst, customer liaison and representative, launch specialist, user trainer, end user, tester, scrum master, product owner, and project manager. Someone who understands the perspective of every stakeholder. That's me.

Strengths and Weaknesses...

I'm honest to a flaw as you're about to read. I enjoy learning new technologies and consider myself a slow learner, but my work ethic and diligence are strong and always help me find my legs. I prefer to understand what I'm coding rather than pasting a snippet that I don't. My professional strengths include software requirements, usability enhancements, debugging, process, and building customer rapport. My weaknesses include UI work - the immediate visual return on my markup tends to lead me all over the page. Other weaknesses include: drinking too much company coffee, and rolling my eyes when youngsters assert themselves as software experts after reading a few blogs. I'd rather spend 8 hours working, than 30 minutes talking about it. Give me a toolbox, I'll dump it out, take a quick inventory, and get the job done as best I can.

Personal Life...

When I'm not learning, working, or coding a side project you can find me with my family, kayak fishing when we get the chance, following our boys' baseball a lot of the time, building furniture when it's not too hot in the garage, hanging out, working out, cooking out, and fixing whatever breaks around the house. I enjoy working with kids of all ages, especially my own. I love my wife (Lynne) of 20+ years, and we're more than proud of our daughter and sons. Samantha is a great writer and artist, very creative, and a free-spirit. Lucas inherited my passion for fishing, while James is showing a solid interest in programming and making crazy videos. I graduated from Texas Tech University in 1999, spent a few years in the panhandle, 7 years in Alabama, a year in Houston, and arrived in the Dallas area in 2010. I plan to be here for a while.
